Scope: `packages/test-runner/src` — auto-sweep harness (contract / auth-enforcement / rate-limit / fuzz), the orphaned CSRF-enforcement layer, the Layer-5 custom-test loader, the stream watcher, the extension registry + registered-layer runtime, and the `runAllTests` orchestrator. ## Verdict summary

All three wave-2 runs agree: **no critical, no live remote/unauth exploit in the package itself.** The dominant live theme is the **"shadow-API wiring gap"** — the CSRF-enforcement layer and the entire `runRegisteredLayers` extension runtime are written/typed/tested but never exported from `index.ts` and never called by `runAllTests`, so the default sweep gives ZERO CSRF coverage and the registry is a write-only façade. The prior HIGH CSRF-header omission in the authenticated sweep layers is unchanged and slightly worse (the `safeMap`/`MUTATING_METHODS` mutating-method partition that limited blast radius was removed in the refactor). A large fraction of wave-1's MEDIUM/LOW findings are now genuinely FIXED in the current tree (verified file:line).

The CSRF-layer being unwired (TR-01) is the single most important live item — flagged prominently below.
